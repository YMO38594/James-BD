NELLIEL'S INTERLUDE

A moment between the former Tres Espada and the All-Father
NELLIEL TU ODELSCHVANCK
"You choose to depart in silence, not ceremony. Why?" Her hazel eyes flash emerald as she searches his face, equal parts curious and concerned.
THE ALL-FATHER
"You know me better than to ask that." A faint smile plays at the corners of his mouth.
NELLIEL
"I know you often forget the weight your absence brings." She gestures toward the sky. "Asgard breathes through your special brand of... madness."
As if on cue, a pink marshmallow creature riding a cotton candy cloud zooms overhead, brandishing a tiny sword as it pursues whatever had stolen its slice of cake. The absurdity of the scene hangs palpably in the air between them.
The two share a laugh, the sound carrying across the golden plains of Asgard. Funnily enough, it wasn't even the strangest sight this week.
That dubious honor belonged to the sentient plant uprising three days prior - an entire army of animated flora that had declared war on a colony of Draugr hiding beneath the Golden Peaks. The conflict had raged for seventy-two hours before Nelliel and Starrk were forced to intervene as reluctant peacekeepers.
The All-Father's only contribution had been to mumble something about "plants versus zombies" before collapsing into uncontrollable laughter.
To this day, the Jameson Jamesonian Randomizer Effect that is built into the very heart of Asgard leaves many observers in a stupor.
But none can deny that it is the most effective defense Asgard has. Aside from the High-King that is.
Shaking herself from her thoughts, Nelliel straightens her posture and meets her King's gaze, awaiting his command.
THE ALL-FATHER
"Travel where I cannot. Speak where I must be mute." His voice carries the weight of prophecy.
Without hesitation, Nelliel drops to one knee, her fist pressed against her heart in salute. When she speaks again, her voice is barely above a whisper, yet it carries the conviction of a sworn oath.
NELLIEL
"I will do as you command... but remember." She lifts her gaze, her eyes now fully emerald, locking onto his form with startling intensity. "Sleipnir is boundless. But even it cannot carry regret." A beat. "Return whole , Father-of-All. Not just alive."
The wind carries her words across the golden fields, where somewhere in the distance, the marshmallow finally reclaims its stolen dessert to the sound of tiny, triumphant cheers.
