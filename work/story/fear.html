THE HERMIT OF THE END
The All-Father opened his eyes to nothing.
No light. No sound. No breath. Only the endless void, stretching beyond time.
Stars burned out. Galaxies crumbled. He remained.
At first, he thought it a test—a trial to endure. But as epochs passed, he understood:
This was his fate.
To outlast everything.
To watch as the last embers of existence flickered and died, leaving only him in the eternal dark.
THE ALL-FATHER
"Alone,"
and the word echoed forever.
________________________________________
[THE DEMIURGE'S VOICE]
From the abyss, it came—a shifting, formless presence that wore a thousand faces, none of them his own.
THE DEMIURGE
"Do you understand now?" the Demiurge asked, its voice the sigh of dying worlds.
The All-Father did not answer.
THE DEMIURGE
"This is your fear," it murmured. "Not death. Not failure. This. To be the last. To be unfinished."
The truth settled into his bones like ice.
He had spent lifetimes fighting, ruling, conquering—all to avoid this moment.
To avoid the silence.
The Demiurge leaned closer, its hollow eyes reflecting the void.
THE DEMIURGE
"You cannot escape it. But you can change it."
________________________________________
[THE CHOICE]
The All-Father looked down at his hands—once calloused from battle, now smooth as stone.
He had wielded power like a blade, thinking it would fill the emptiness.
But the emptiness was not outside him.
It was in the way he refused to end.
A god could not die.
But a man could.
And so, he reached into the void—and rewrote the rules of existence itself.
THE ALL-FATHER
"Let there be cycles," he commanded.
"Let life lead to death. Let death lead to life. Let souls reunite, even in different forms."
The Demiurge screamed as the first spark of reincarnation ignited—a tiny, fragile light in the dark.
Somewhere, in a world not yet born, a child took its first breath.
________________________________________
[EPILOGUE: THE NAME]
The Demiurge writhed before him, its form collapsing into something smaller, quieter.
THE DEMIURGE
"You would choose mortality?" it hissed.
THE ALL-FATHER
"I choose meaning."
And with that, he stepped into the cycle—no longer a king, no longer a god.
Just a man.
