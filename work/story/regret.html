THE CHAINS OF REGRET
The All-Father opened his eyes—and found himself powerless.
He floated like a ghost through the ruins of his own past, forced to watch as every failure, every drop of spilled blood, played out before him.
A child wept over a corpse. A city burned. Friends turned their backs.
THE ALL-FATHER
"Stop,"
But his voice was silent.
He reached to intervene—but his hands passed through the world like smoke.
This was his punishment.
To see. To remember. To ache—without redemption.

[THE VISION OF KEITA]
The final memory unfolded—a broken man kneeling in the rain.
KEITA SHIMIMURA
Himself, before the power, before the throne.
Keita clutched his chest, where his soul rotted from the weight of his sins. His fingers dug into the mud, smearing it with blood that never washed away.
KEITA SHIMIMURA
"I'm sorry... I'm sorry..."
But no one answered.
The scene twisted. Keita's body contorted, his screams turning guttural as bone-white masks erupted across his skin. He thrashed—not fighting the transformation, but welcoming it.
Anything to escape the pain.
A serpent's tail lashed. A hollow's maw split wide.
A snake, shedding its past.
________________________________________
[THE DEMIURGE'S WHISPER]
"Do you see now?"
The voice came from everywhere—from the blood in the soil, from the hollow's hollow eyes.
THE DEMIURGE
The Demiurge took shape before him, its form a shifting mirror of every face he'd ever worn: king, warrior, monster.
THE DEMIURGE
"Regret is not your chains," it murmured. "It is your anchor."
The All-Father stared at the serpent that had once been Keita.
THE ALL-FATHER
"I didn't want to remember,"
THE DEMIURGE
"That is why you are here. To choose: drown in what you were... or let the past die."
The serpent lifted its head. For a moment, its eyes held something human.
Something forgiving.
________________________________________
[THE CHOICE]
The All-Father reached out—not to stop the transformation, but to bless it.
THE ALL-FATHER
"You suffered enough,"
THE DEMIURGE
"And you?"
A breath. A pause.
THE ALL-FATHER
"I let go."
The chains shattered.
________________________________________
[EPILOGUE: THE NAME]
The Demiurge coiled around him, its form softening into something almost kind.
THE DEMIURGE
"Who are you now?"
The All-Father looked down at his hands—no longer spectral, no longer stained.
THE ALL-FATHER
"The one who remembers,"
He said.
THE ALL-FATHER
"But does not linger."
And with that, he stepped forward—into the light, into the unknown.
