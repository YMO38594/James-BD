STARRK'S INTERLUDE

A moment between the Primera Espada and the All-Father
COYOTE STARRK
"So you're going off to die. Again." His voice carries its usual languid disinterest, but there's an edge beneath the apathy.
Starrk leans against a golden pillar, hiding in its shadow. His posture, the picture of indifference. As if he was done with the world and everything in it. And with the flora fiasco earlier that week, he had every right to be. But his golden eyes track the All-Father's movements with an unnatural focus.
THE ALL-FATHER
"No." The word lands with finality. "This time, I'm going off to live. For the first time."
COYOTE STARRK
(shaking his head) "Sounds poetic. You've been spending too much time around Nell."
THE ALL-FATHER
"You're in command while I'm gone." The air grows heavy with reiatsu. "Don't watch over me, Heimdall. Watch over them."
The shift is instantaneous. Starrk's habitual slouch straightens as if pulled by strings, his golden eyes widening as they lock onto his King.
The All-Father had always carried himself with quiet authority, a whimsical demeanor that put even the most battle-hardened warriors at ease. But there was not a trace of it now. And it showed in his voice. Powerful and domineering, yet always being used to say the most ridiculous things. His voice was always more suited towards a semi-tyrannical Overlord than a man who boxed with a gillian the size of Hueco Mundo for fun.
For the first time, Starrk feels the full force of his King's power pressing against his senses - not as an attack, but as a command that bypasses conscious thought and speaks directly to the soul.
COYOTE STARRK
Pushing Heimdall further then he ever has before, Starrk pierces the veil of time and says..
"I see a hundred thousand futures with every breath." His voice drops to a whisper. "Most of them... end with you hanging."
THE ALL-FATHER
"And some?" There's no fear in the question, only curiosity.
COYOTE STARRK
"Some don't." A ghost of his usual smirk appears. "I'll take that as hope."
With that, Starrk closes his eyes and turns inward. Centuries of battle, of loneliness, of reluctant leadership - all distilled into this single moment of decision.
He finds what he seeks buried deep - changed by time, but fundamentally unbroken. The sensation that follows is both familiar and alien: his soul splitting once more. But where once it had been an act of desperate isolation, now it is a promise.
When he opens his eyes, he extends his arms toward his King. Cradled within them: a tiny, squirming bundle - a newborn child with wisps of dark hair and golden eyes that already seem far too knowing.
THE ALL-FATHER
(deadpan) "If my problems could be dealt with by diapers and ear-piercing wails, I would have made sure you were the first to know."
The most powerful being in existence receives a sharp smack to the back of his head for his troubles.
COYOTE STARRK
(equally deadpan) "For a supposedly omniscient guy, you are really stupid." He gently retrieves the now-crying infant. "When you come back, do so with a name for this little one as well, okay?"
Without waiting for a response, Starrk turns on his heel and walks away, the child cradled protectively against his chest. Stepping out of the pillar's shadow, and into the warm, golden-light.
